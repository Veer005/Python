Gravel Driveways & Driveway Grading

Gravel and dirt driveways can be a great choice for longer lanes, rural properties, and areas where full asphalt isn’t necessary. The key is proper grading and drainage so the surface stays usable and sheds water instead of washing out.

Gravel driveway installation

We install new gravel driveways and improve existing ones, focusing on base preparation and water flow. The goal is a driveway that’s easy to drive on and holds up to weather.

  • Assessment of existing ground conditions and slope.
  • Grading and compaction of the driveway base.
  • Delivery and spreading of gravel to the right depth.
  • Shaping the surface so water runs off instead of pooling.
Gravel driveways with proper grading for drainage and long-term use.

Driveway grading & reshaping

Even a good gravel driveway can develop ruts or low spots over time. We regrade and reshape driveways so they drain properly and are easier to maintain.

  • Filling washouts and low areas that collect water.
  • Re-establishing a crown or slope where needed.
  • Blending new material into the existing driveway.
  • Optional asphalt “apron” at the road to reduce erosion.
For many properties, a well-built gravel driveway with the right grading can be a durable, cost-effective solution for years.
